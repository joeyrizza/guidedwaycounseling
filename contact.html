Frequently Asked Questions

What happens in the first session?

The first session is a time for us to get to know each other. We'll discuss what brings you to therapy, your goals, your history, and how I can best support you. I'll answer any questions you have about the therapeutic process. This is also a chance for you to see if we're a good fit.

How long does therapy take?

This varies for each person. Some clients find relief in a few months, while others benefit from longer-term support. We'll regularly check in about your progress and goals to ensure therapy continues to meet your needs.

Is telehealth as effective as in-person?

Yes! Research shows that telehealth therapy is just as effective as in-person for most concerns. It offers the added convenience of attending from your own comfortable space. I'm experienced in both formats and will ensure you receive quality care regardless of which option you choose.

Do you take insurance?

I am currently a private-pay practice. However, I can provide you with a superbill (receipt) that you may be able to submit to your insurance for potential out-of-network reimbursement. I also offer a sliding scale for those facing financial hardship.

What if I need to cancel?

Life happens! I ask that you provide 24-hour notice if you need to cancel or reschedule. This allows me to offer the time to another client who may be waiting. Late cancellations may be subject to a fee.

Is our conversation confidential?

Yes, confidentiality is a cornerstone of therapy. What you share stays between us, with a few legal exceptions (such as imminent harm to yourself or others, child abuse, or court orders). I'll fully explain the limits of confidentiality in our first session.

Do I need to have a specific diagnosis?

No! You don't need a diagnosis to benefit from therapy. Many people seek support simply because they're going through a difficult time, want to grow, or need someone to talk to. Therapy is for anyone who wants support in navigating life's challenges.

What if I'm not sure therapy is right for me?

That's completely normal! The free 15-minute consultation is designed for exactly this. It's a no-pressure way to ask questions, share concerns, and get a feel for whether working together makes sense for you.
